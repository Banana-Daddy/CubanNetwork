Executive summary

The three findings that change how you approach this market.

Correction #1

Cuban is a passive investor, not an operator. He co-owns the Dallas Flash but makes zero operational or technology decisions. Mark Molthan — the team's Managing Director — is the gateway. Every pitch aimed at "Cuban's pickleball team" must go through Molthan first.

Correction #2

The most valuable facility in Cuban's orbit is Banner House at T Bar M — a $70M luxury club with 45+ courts across tennis, pickleball, and padel — and it has zero visible booking technology. Jessica Nowitzki (Dirk's wife) curates the art program there.

Correction #3

Cuban has no confirmed padel investments. Low-quality sources conflate pickleball with padel. However, Banner House's 5 padel courts and the growing Texas padel market create indirect exposure through his network.

Biggest opportunity

Tom Dundon owns the entire pro pickleball stack — leagues, tours, e-commerce, media, software — but has two clear gaps: facility operations and team engagement platforms. Those gaps are exactly where MatchFlow lives.
